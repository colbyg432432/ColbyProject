Holding The Golf Club

Holding a golf club properly is essential for a good swing, and Ben Hogan's advice on grip is timeless. As I position my hands on the club, I recall Hogan's emphasis on a firm yet relaxed grip. He advised placing the left hand under the shaft, ensuring that the back of the hand faces the target and the grip rests on the first joint of the index finger. This creates a stable foundation. I wrap my fingers around the grip and place the pad of my left palm on top of the grip, allowing for better control. The thumb and forefinger should form a 'V' pointing towards the right shoulder. This positioning is vital for enabling the wrists to hinge naturally during the swing.

Then, I integrate my right hand, as Hogan suggested, by placing it below the left, with the grip lying across the fingers. The lifeline of my right palm covers the left thumb, and I gently wrap the fingers around the club. The 'V' formed by the right thumb and forefinger should mirror the left, also pointing towards the right shoulder. Hogan emphasized that this overlap or interlock grip creates a unified hand action, crucial for a fluid and powerful swing. It's a delicate balance between maintaining a firm grip and ensuring my hands aren't tense, as tension could hinder the natural flow of the swing. Remembering Hogan's guidance, I feel more confident and connected to the club, ready to execute a swing with precision and grace.
